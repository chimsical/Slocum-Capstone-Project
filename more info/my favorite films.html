My Favorite Films

A good majority of the following examples fall into the horror genre, and some viewing experiences are not-safe-for-work. Despite this, I enjoy them deeply for their artistic value.

  • "ParaNorman", 2012

    Stop-motion animation is one of my ideal artistic mediums for story-telling, and I consider "ParaNorman" one of the best examples of it. This movie is the first ever stop motion film to use full-color 3-D printers, which are now instrumental in a lot of big-budget stop-motion puppet making (like "Guillermo del Toro's Pinocchio" 2022).

  • "Hereditary", 2018

    This movie solidified Ari Aster as one of my favorite filmmakers; he writes and directs all of his films, which gives him great control over the story being portrayed. "Hereditary" is a great representation of a horror film where the horror can be interpreted as being both literal and symbolic — are the demons that haunt this family real, or is the way each family member's affected by the demonic presence a visual representation of the way they are affected by their grief?

  • "Suspiria", 2018

    I generally dislike remakes of classic movies, but this version of "Suspira" is more of a homage to the original film. Compared to the brightly colored setting of the original, this film provided the artistic aspect through the contemporary ballet sequences, and was genuinely horrifying overall. I do also enjoy the original "Suspira" from 1977, but for very different reasons.

  • Special Mention - "Opal", short film by Jack Stauber, 2020

    An absolutely incredible mixed-media stop-motion musical film with a story that still haunts me.
